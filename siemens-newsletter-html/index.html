FROM LEADERS BEYOND MOBILITY PUNE

Pankaj Vyas,
CEO & Managing Director, Siemens Technology and Services Pvt. Ltd.
Dear colleagues,
Growing together, connecting communities is an apt theme for this edition of the Mobility newsletter. Thephrase couldn’t be more apt–given the present times. The coronavirus outbreak has dramatically changed our lives including our relationships with other people in our communities, our families, our homes, and our workplace. We have missed seeing everyone as we try to keep each other safe.

The positive side is we have found ourselves spending more time with those who share our home, whether family, housemates, or both- not to mention pets. Despite the downside of the pandemic- we are also witnessing rays of hope in various walks of life with widespread outpouring of charity, togetherness, and empathy for strangers. This has redefined what ‘community’ means in the current times.

Throughout this crisis, technology has become the connective tissue that binds us together, from video calls to new tech ideas. Be it at our homes or in industries, we are increasingly turning to technological innovators like you to build resilience. If social distancing is the norm now, technology appears to be the medicine that connects us now.

This is clearly seen in the way you adapted to the crisis, transitioned to an entirely virtual environment dealing with the isolation due to the high number of cases that Pune faced. It’s been tough, but in all this you always kept the focus on the customer and the deliverables you had.

From welcoming new colleagues virtually and finding innovative ways to welcome them to Siemens, to growing together as a team by taking additional responsibilities to support colleagues in different regions and bringing more responsibility by way of new work and business for Siemens Advanta Development in India. In all this, you also reached a

milestone of being a 500 strong team! You’ve been resilient in every way and for this, I’d like to thank you all.

With the strong foundation that you all have built, I am very confident that the Mobility team will continue to create tremendous value for Siemens and its customers. Thank you for all your hardwork and a big thank you to your families for their support in these challenging times! Looking forward to more success stories coming from the Mobility team! Stay Safe, Stay Healthy!

LEADERSHIP MUSINGS

01

In March 2020, we decided that it’s not a question of whether we ‘can’ or not pull this off but a question of ‘how’ we should pull this off. Together, we decided that we’d set a completely virtual team of the MO-IT India Hub–even when it was an unexplored path for us.

Now that I look back, I can say with certainty that we took the right decision. In close cooperation with the local HR and talent acquisition team, with the help of innovative IT solutions, we succeeded in conducting 500+ virtual job interviews. Through that, we onboarded 40 colleagues in less than 6 months. However, onboarding our colleagues were just the beginning. The real challenge was to cultivate team-spirit and smoothen collaboration in a team that’s never even seen each other.

To address this, we at Siemens Mobility IT India Hub started multiple initiatives to boost interaction. We started with the buddy-program wherein a buddy was assigned to every new member to interact with and to reach out for help. In addition to that, we chalked out a 50/100 days plan for each individual to help them focus their efforts in the right direction. To further aid our new colleagues, we organized multiple knowledge transfer sessions from industry experts. Lastly, to ease their relationship with their team members and foster a feeling of togetherness, we organized virtual get-togethers, monthly team events, family appreciation meets, German language lessons.

Our efforts bore fruits sooner than we had expected. Within 3 months of joining, most of our new colleagues were able to take on responsibilities in various areas such as ERP activities, PLM space, global infrastructure, and CRM projects.

We have several other initiatives planned to continue building the spirit of collaboration within the team. All our efforts are towards a shared vision: to seamlessly integrate into the global team and enable Mobility IT to grow by providing additional capacity and relevant expertise.

Ultimately, we aim to deliver innovative and high-quality products and become a trusted business partner. By building a team of more than 40 people fully virtually in less than 6 months we demonstrated capabilities to “walk the talk” at Mobility IT India Hub.

We’ve established that we deliver on our commitments and we grow stronger together. A testament to this is the fact that we have been receiving countless referrals from our new team members for open positions at the Hub. Clearly, our comrades are enjoying the environment we’ve built and trust us enough to help us grow by recommending suitable profiles. All said and done, our journey has just begun. The Mobility IT India Hub is well supported by the entire senior management of Siemens Mobility.
