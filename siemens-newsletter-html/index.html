Introducing Growth Talks

In its simplest form, a Growth Talk is purely a future-focused exchange between people that results in learning and progress toward a goal. The shared objectives of all exchanges is to be development-oriented and performance enhancing.

We replace our system-driven annual processes with engaging, jointly-driven conversations that focus on individual growth and drive business success. These conversations and exchanges can be approached in many different ways, depending on the situation.

  • Confirming alignment of work toward achieving strategic business goals.

  • Discussions about skill sets, career aspirations, and/or development opportunities.
  • Timely, tailored, and specific recognition of contributions or personal successes.

Growth in VUCA times

Shrikant Vachaspati,
HR Business Partner Manager, STSPL

What does growth mean and why it is important is a question that we ask ourselves so very often. The drive for achieving bigger goals and moving ahead has been a constant human endeavor. And it does not matter what level you peg your goals; the aim is to do better than what you are doing now. There are many forces that work on us in influencing this growth, both internal and external. And in a world that is now VUCA (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, and Ambiguity) in its nature, the external forces have multiplied, both in number and impact.

As the Red Queen said in Alice In Wonderland, you need to run fast to stay in the same place. And if you want to reach someplace else, you need to run twice as fast.

Traditional Growth has placed the onus on the organization, the managers to provide direction. Growth was time bound and skill building was one dimensional. But with an ever changing environment, there are aspects in terms of mind shifts and behavior that are essential to harness the potential of growth in the VUCA world. Let us examine a few of them -

  • Employees need to take ownership to lead the way by developing their capacity, constantly deepening their self-awareness. They should routinely challenge their understanding of themselves, their strengths, opportunities, interests and values, extracting lessons from challenges and failures, and update their understanding of the unique value proposition they have to offer.
  • As employees deepen their self-awareness and develop the habit of reflection, it becomes incumbent upon leaders to understand and act on this information and insights. This happens through conversations that are an ongoing dialogue and routine interactions. This is what at Siemens we have adapted in the form of Growth Talks.
  • With VUCA in play, career-development plans can no longer be cast in concrete. Employees need to play out multiple possibilities concurrently, holding each lightly and being willing to change as necessary. This calls for an agile outlook at career opportunities and growth.
  • Employees and leaders will move heavily into informal or unstructured learning (which includes development experiences, mentoring, visibility, and networking) to offer on-the-spot or targeted high-impact actions that work within the employee’s (versus the organization’s) timeline.
  • Fitting development into already overwhelming workloads can be challenging. It’s easy for employees to let their growth and learning take a backseat to more urgent concerns. We need to ensure that the environment at work encourages employees to work their developmental intentions, by acknowledging the acquisition of new skills or experiences. With the new Global Learning Platform, Siemens has made it easier for employees to digitally learn.

Careers exist in today’s VUCA environment; but career development and growth continue to thrive even in these challenging conditions. With COVID and a largely work from home environment that is likely to continue for some more time to come, the use of digital platforms to learn new skills, and virtually come together for innovation offers tremendous opportunity for employees and leaders who are willing to make the process more versatile, uplifting, choice-filled and active.

WORDS OF WISDOM

Dr. Anand Nadkarni,
Siemens Technology and Services Pvt. Ltd.

How many times we have looked at famous leaders with awe and admiration. Forgetting that we personalize this virtue almost subconsciously... For us, the leadership and persona of an individual become one. The problem with this perspective is that we divide our pack into ‘Leaders’ and ‘Followers’ as if it's a lifelong fate and faith that is rigid and unchangeable.

But ‘to lead’ is a function, a role that can be very dynamic, the style can change, the person can change and also there can be a role reversal. A leader for a particular task, project, or responsibility can be a follower in the next endeavour. In today's world, ‘leadership’ needs to be a ‘Functional Proactivity’ based on a person’s mastery of that particular skill, quality or aptitude. Hence, leadership does not become an exclusive role but an inclusive responsibility.

In 1930, just before Gandhiji started the epic Salt March, he distributed regional and local responsibilities to the last layer of workers, clearly stating who was to replace whom in case the initial leaders got arrested. Someone asked him, “In case all your batches of leaders get arrested, then?” “The battle itself will throw-up new leaders”, said Gandhiji. It was his faith that each person has a leader within, and if committed to a goal, those hidden pearls start shining.

By September 1930, when the entire leadership was behind bars, the chain of satyagraha continued till January 1931 by heroes, unheard of till then. For any organisation, this is an important example. As a culture, we are more prone to looking at particular charismatic figures and personalities as ‘saviours’.

By September 1930, when the entire leadership was behind bars, the chain of satyagraha continued till January 1931 by heroes, unheard of till then. For any organisation, this is an important example. As a culture, we are more prone to looking at particular charismatic figures and personalities as ‘saviours’.

In a famous sloka from the Gita, the ‘me’ is not a person but ‘divine leadership’ getting expressed through many people.

Let us ‘normalize’ leadership. Let the last person in an organisation inculcate his own touch of leadership.... his own expression of the divine essence. The process of actively leading can be extremely educative and thrilling, though at times burdensome. But it can give a golden touch to an otherwise mundane life. We have seen countless times that during times of crisis, unknown people stand tall and deliver beyond their capabilities.

It's my humble contention that the future belongs to a collective leadership that respects the value of interdependence. In fact ‘leadership for interdependence’ is a function so clearly seen in nature. The leaves of a plant ‘lead’ photosynthesis, the flowers ‘lead’ pollen spread, and the roots ‘lead’ the nutritional function.

Without competing they lead the tree towards blossoming and optimising its potential. And when we own the responsibility, we actually start enjoying the very experience of leadership. When all these functional leaders help the tree express its ‘TREENESS’ in its totality, each function also gets optimally fulfilled. Interestingly none of these ‘functional leaders’ ask for a separate acknowledgment. They merge their aspirations with the whole ‘TREENESS’.

Civilizations have survived on these critical expressions by unknown leaders. It's time we underline and revisit this fact in the current stream of extreme changes. That will add an interesting angle to effective coping for all of us.

This will help the diagnosis, the clearance of faults, and alarm management.

"Alone we can do so little, together we can do so much."

2020 has been that year which taught us the importance of the above quote. It has completely changed the way most of us work. Our relationships with our colleagues have been redefined. Going for lunch together, having a cup of tea at the local tapri, celebrating birthdays – usually, these were the moments that helped us grow from a group of individuals to a team. Covid-19 has prevented us from enjoying such real-life moments, and it seems it might continue to do so for a while. However, this does not mean that we stop growing and connecting.

The initial days of work from home were a huge adjustment, but we all learned to adapt ourselves. As part of this journey, we moved our day-to-day practices from the real world to this virtual world. Being part of a team meant a lot of things. A day in the office for our team generally started with the daily scrum meeting. Our first order of business was to conduct this online. Tracking this in such a way that the status is visible to all the stakeholders was very important.

We now have a daily 15-minute standup facilitated by our Scrum Master, wherein each team member gives an update of the task they are working on using the RTC dashboard. In the absence of the scrum master, any team member steps up to conduct the same. This is true for all the agile ceremonies such as the Sprint review, Sprint retrospective and backlog grooming.

It has been a continuous learning process to time box ourselves. However, we feel that the daily standup is essential for us as this is a required human connection we need to kick start our day.

During the past year, we also had a successful release. This would not have been possible without the entire team's joint effort. In the beginning, it was a bit overwhelming to figure out how to work together virtually. We had a bit of a shaky start, but finally, we set ourselves a new rhythm.

We learnt to break the tasks down into smaller stories to achieve better sprint velocity, spend more time in understanding the requirements together so that we could work on them individually, squinted away at the fine print-

of a screen shared during a call to discuss and debug solutions all the while keeping the goal of the release in mind. Siemens also ensured that we could grow individually by giving us access to different pieces of training and certifications. Team members have completed various activities such as IMS, AI/ML, etc.

Being at home unable to interact with others does take its toll, though. Different people went through different emotional upheavals. However, we have always tried to maintain an open communication line, be it among ourselves or with our managers. We had talks wherein each team member openly spoke about the difficulties and challenges they were facing, and we tried figuring out how we could make things better for them. Every day we have a DND ( Do not disturb) time zone wherein we keep aside an hour roughly. It is the me-time for each individual wherein he/she is free to do whatever they want. It could be completing some housework, resting for some time or even just completing an individual task without having anyone disturb them at that time.

Vehicle Actuated Signals

Jayant Wagh,
Advanta DS MO

Traffic congestion is becoming a critical issue day by day. This is due to the increased urbanization, industrialization, and vehicle density. Sometimes emergency vehicles like ambulances and fire trucks are stuck in traffic and unable to reach in time for rescue.

The worst traffic jams or long queues occur at intersections where cars, bikes, buses, or pedestrians cross the road. If we can manage traffic at such intersections, we might resolve this issue significantly. Almost all big cities have traffic signals and a dedicated traffic police force for managing everyday traffic. In case of emergencies, the police can use a traffic control panel to make dynamic signal changes or take control over intersection traffic manually.

"Vehicle-Actuated Signals" is one of the preferred methods for optimizing intersection traffic. There are also various detectors used for actuation. e.g., inductive loop detectors, radar, RFID, or cameras.

Signal controller programs take these detector actuations as input from all approach lanes and dynamically adjust the signal timing plan and reduce queue length on all lanes as much as possible.

These steps help reduce congestion time significantly. Some cities have preferred special vehicle detection mechanisms to detect emergency vehicles and public transport vehicles to prioritize cross intersections.

Typically, traffic signal controllers operate in three modes.

Pre-Timed Signal: In this mode, a traffic signal's Green-Amber-Red timing is fixed, and these patterns are repeated throughout the day. Sometimes, these fixed timing partners or designs are based on historical traffic scenarios. An example can be giving more time to the green signals in the morning, especially those that lead to offices or industrial areas. This will ensure the smooth movement of traffic.

Actuated Signal: In this method, one can use vehicle detectors to detect traffic based on queue length, vehicle type, and priority; based on these factors, signal controllers can optimize signal timing dynamically. Taking the same scenario as above, if there is more traffic moving towards the office or industrial area, a lane could get maximum green time dynamically.

Mixed-Mode: One can use this mode at major and minor traffic road junctions. Typically, major roads have fixed timing, and minor roads get actuated signals. By doing this, minor roads will get a green signal when there is sizable traffic. Otherwise, you end up prioritizing just major-road traffic.

Advantages of Actuated Signals

  • They can reduce congestion/delay (if properly timed).
  • They are adaptable to short-term fluctuations in traffic flow.
  • They can provide continuous operation under low volume conditions.
  • They are especially useful at multiple phase intersections.

Disadvantages of Actuated Signals

  • If the traffic demand pattern is very regular, the extra benefit of adding local actuation is minimal or non-existent.
  • Installation cost is two to three times the cost of a pre-timed signal installation.
  • Actuated controllers are much more complicated than pre-timed controllers, increasing maintenance costs.
  • They require careful inspection & maintenance to ensure proper operation.

Traffic engineers are coming up with newer concepts like Signal Synchronization, which allows coordinating traffic signals along a single route so that vehicles get a progressive green signal at each junction. This is another crucial aspect that we will discuss more in the upcoming newsletter.

Creating a Playbook — Know-How:

I began my first playbook creation by converting the existing Network Shell script for product installation on a virtual machine in a Cloud environment to a playbook. Playbooks are expressed in YAML format and mostly consist of a series of sequential ‘tasks’. At a very basic level, a task is nothing more than a call to an Ansible module, which is a predefined set of system commands, resources, etc. To have an overview of playbook creation, here is the official link to the documentation: AnsiblePlaybooks

Steps to execute playbook:

  1. The first step in executing a playbook is to check for any syntax errors while writing the playbook

  2. Below is the step to execute a playbook 
    * ansible-playbook test.yml
  3. For detailed debugging step by step for a playbook use this command:
    * ansible-playbook test.yml -- step -vv

Troubleshooting a playbook:

The most common errors faced while executing a playbook are:

1. No such file or directory

— Usually, for this kind of error, you need to look if the file is executable by checking the permissions of the file.

— Another thing to check is if you are running the playbook as a root user or any other non-root Ansible user which has the required privileges to execute the playbook.

— Another basic error could be that the file is not copied properly to the target server before execution

2. Permissions denied

— You may need to check if the user ID you’re using to execute the playbook on the server has the correct permissions to execute the playbook or if you need to root otherwise.

    Magnification in train control system

    Metro Signalling & Train control system technology focus means of reducing headway, ensuring safety, and improving efficiency and reduction in the cost of operation. The Signalling & Train Control system on Main Line Railways and Metros are different on account of very different traffic requirements besides civil and E&M infrastructure. Signalling & Train Control in metros not only ensures train safety, but it also integrates, interfaces, and automates areas of operation, driving, passenger information, collection of information, and analysis of the same. Signalling & Train Control systems have been dominated using Track Circuit-based Signalling & Train Control for more than five decades.

    The success of Metro rail systems lies in their ability to provide frequent, fast, safe, and comfortable journeys in the urban conglomeration, not only to the regular commuters but also to the occasional traveler or tourist alike. Signalling and Train Control systems play a major role in achieving these objectives. While enhancing operational efficiency by providing control systems to achieve the target headway, it also always ensures the total safety of the train movements besides adding value to other systems by the on-line interface.

    The rapid developments in the computer and microcircuit technology, as well as telecommunication, have led to the development of Integrated Metro Control systems, in which computers (including failsafe forms of computers) fulfill many of the decision making roles traditionally performed by Train operators, station controllers, Traffic controllers, etc., eventually leading to the introduction of highly automated Metro Rail with driverless trains.

    Need for Train detection and methods used:

    To convey information to a train about where to stop and at what speed to run required all the trains on any line to be detected automatically by the system. This was achieved by the invention of the so-called “Track circuits”. Track circuits were first invented in the USA (DC Track circuits) in 1872 and later picked up and improved by British railways. London Underground was the first large-scale user in a Metro rail network 1904-06.

    The need to provide information to train resulted in the invention of “Coded Track circuits” with modulation of a basic AC carrier with codes. These required insulated joints between sections of rails like earlier track circuits for demarcating the individual track circuit blocks. Later came the “Jointless Track circuits” which use audio frequencies and provide high impedance between adjacent track circuits by a tuning process thus avoiding the need for insulated joints. These are commonly called Audio frequency Track Circuits (AFTC) and these help in transmitting codes that modulate the basic AFTC carrier which in turn are picked up by an antenna suspended below the train.

    In addition to track circuits, another system called “Axle counter” is also used for train detection. In this method, axles are detected through a magnetic circuit linking a transmitter and a receiver across the rail, and whenever a metallic axle interposes the received signal drops are detected and counted as one pulse. In this manner, all the axles coming into one block are counted at the entry and if all the axles are counted out at the exit of the block the block is declared as clear.

    In the latest advancement in Metro signalling, a system called Communication based train control (CBTC) has been in use for more than a decade. In this system, the primary train detection is purely based on a failsafe communication link between the train and the control center with the train communicating its position continuously and the control Centre communicates the position to other trains for maintaining the safety distance between two trains. Track circuits or axle counters if any are used only as a secondary detection in case the Metro operator desires a fallback system.

    Characteristics of Communication-Based Train Control System (CBTC)

    Communications-Based Train Control (CBTC) is a railway signalling system that makes use of the telecommunications between the train and track equipment for traffic management and train control. CBTC systems enable, the exact position of a train is known more accurately than with the traditional signalling systems, which results more efficiently and safely to manage the railway traffic and improve headway.

    CBTC systems allow different levels of automation or Grades of Automation, GoA, as classified in the IEC 62290-1. CBTC is not a synonym for “driverless” or “automated trains” although it is considered as a basic technology for this purpose.

    Driverless Train Operation (DTO) and Unattended Train Operation (UTO):

    More sophisticated systems free the driver from the need to be at the front of the train – referred to as Driverless Train Operation (DTO) or GOA level 3. In DTO the driver can move away from the front of the train but remains available to provide customer-facing duties and to drive the train in the event of a failure of the DTO/ATO system.

    As the driver is no longer able to see the route ahead this imposes a greater demand on guideway security and platform controls.

    Platform screen doors must be provided, to allow ATO to handle door closing also on the signal from ATP that all the Platform Screen Doors have closed fully.

    In DTO, train doors and train departure from a station platform may also be controlled automatically or manually from a location other than a driver cab at the front of the train. ATO can be given the operating instructions by the ATS regarding dwell time and hold for regulating the train on platforms as required by the operational situation and in the normal course, the driver wherever he happens to be on the train is relieved of all operational workload.

    CCTV information of the inside of coaches is transmitted live in new systems to OCC for controllers to observe for any untoward incidents inside the moving train and with other advancements in prevention and detection of unauthorized access to the viaduct or tunnel, even a separate train operator is no longer required on the cab in the train. The space required for the equipment could also be saved and passenger capacity increased at the end cabs. Thus, it became possible to have an Unattended Train Operation. Driverless ATO without an on-board attendant is referred to as Unattended Train Operation (UTO) or GOA level 4. UTO can range from empty train movements ( only to a siding, or in an automated depot, for example) to the operation of trains in passenger service with no attendant on board. The latter requires that the reliability of the systems has to be of a higher level and the train can be operated remotely under failure conditions, or at the minimum can be reached by shore-based personal in a short period.

    Passengers need to be reassured and hence good communication and CCTV links between the vehicle and an informed staff member at OCC or a specified location are essential.

    Benefits of CBTC

    CBTC makes effective use of infrastructure in the following ways

    • Allows trains at closer headways.
    • Greater precision and control of trains is possible.
    • Provides continuous safe train separation & over-speed protection.
    • Utilizes the track most efficiently while ensuring safety.

    CBTC requires less maintenance cost due to:

    • Driverless system (or upgradable to driverless) to reduce operating costs.
    • Less wayside equipment
    • Improved reliability
    • Real-time diagnostic data

    Risks of CBTC

    The primary risk of a CBTC system is that if the communications link between any of the trains is disrupted then all or part of the system might have to enter a failsafe state until the problem is remedied. Depending on the severity of the communication loss, this state can range from vehicles temporarily reducing speed, coming to a halt, or operating in a degraded mode until communications are re-established.

    Communications failures can result from equipment malfunction, electromagnetic interference, weak signal strength or saturation of the communications medium. In this case, an interruption can result in a service brake or emergency brake application as real-time situational awareness is a critical safety requirement for CBTC and if these interruptions are frequent enough it could seriously impact service.

    Cyber-physical challenge is the severe risk in this system. CBTC Systems that make use of open standards for wireless digital communications link have a much larger attack surface and can be subject to various types of hacking including intrusion of the communications network and tampering with safety-critical messages that, in the worst case, could result in a safety hazard.

    CBTC systems may be designed with centralized supervision systems, to improve maintainability and reduce installation costs. If so, there is an increased risk of a single point of failure that could disrupt service over an entire system or line.

    Every big move on a new platform comes with a price tag. Saving on cloud maintenance holds a huge cost appeal, but migration can be so much more than that since migration projects run on three different costs – On-premises, Online, and Project and the efficiency saving comes with early ramp-down of legacy environment.

    With an accelerated project approach, we were able to bring down the costs by nearly 31%.

    The Accelerated Approach

    We decided to onboard SharePoint consultants from Infosys and IBM, helping us migrate medium and highly complex sites in parallel with the partner appointed by Siemens IT – Atos – to migrate simple sites. It also became easier to define the right priorities to migrate first, starting from sites that required minimal effort but generated high cost.

    Another major contributor was an efficient housekeeping survey performed via a small application, which resulted in the deletion of 20% of sites that were no longer in use.

    Communication and structured migration processes played a critical role, keeping our users up-to-date and offering a central application to get the live migration status of their respective sites.

    All the different strategies, plans, and actions resulted in 50% ram-down already - a pioneer among all Siemens organizations.
