Do You Find Yourself Drowning In All Your Data With No Sign Of The Shore?

The problem with data is, it has a way of piling up, very very quickly. Without employing proper management and storage methodologies you’ll find yourself with a whole world of Data but no way to sort through it. Then there’s the problem of scalability. The longer your business functions, the more data it accrues and all of that Data needs to be stored somewhere, somewhere from where you can easily retrieve it.

While most businesses have a desire for data-driven insight, many are not realising that ambition. The result is that data management is often fragmented and driven by multiple stakeholders. This leaves organisations dealing with a high degree of inaccurate and disparate data.

Problems

1. Excessive Amounts Of Data

Every day, it’s estimated that 2.5 quintillion bytes of data are created and that’s just today. Imagine what years and years of data will look like.

All this data needs to be stored somewhere, a place which does not have a cap for the amount of data you can store.

2. Lack of Processes and Systems

Without proper processes and systems, you will inevitably have data inconsistencies whenever you extract data from disparate databases. The end result will be that nobody trusts the numbers and you will have to double-check and triple-check it every time you form a conclusion from all the data.

3. Fragmented Data Ownership

A lack of data ownership is one of the key shortfalls for most organisations today. Data ownership is still predominantly fragmented, with the management of data quality driven by multiple stakeholders and frequently measured at a department-by-department level, rather than across the entire business as a whole.

In view of all this, it might be wise to seek professional help.
