Concerned About Network's Security?

Many companies suffer from numerous network security problems without ever actually realizing it. What’s worse, when these problems go unresolved, they can create openings for attackers to breach a company’s security infrastructure to steal data and generally wreak havoc.

Of course, it doesn’t necessarily need to be hackers or malware either. Network security can often be severely compromised by unintentional human errors as well.

One of your biggest threats, even though you might not have thought about it are your employees themselves. About 60% of all attacks were carried out by insiders themselves. Whether it’s intentional leaks and misuse of account privileges, or identity theft arising from a phishing campaign or other social engineering attack that compromises their user account data, the people inside your business represent one of the biggest security problems you’ll ever face.

Because these threats come from trusted users and systems, they’re also among the hardest to identify and stop.

However, with proper network security protocols in place, you’ll be able to identify and ward off all possible threats and inadvertent mistakes.

We Provide

1. Firewalls

Firewalls are always the first line of defense when it comes to network security. Firewalls put up a barrier between your trusted internal network and untrusted outside networks, such as the Internet. They use a set of defined rules to allow or block traffic. A firewall can be hardware, software, or both. Investing in a strong firewall will help you identify threats and completely prevent unauthorized intrusions.

2. Bandwidth Throttling

Bandwidth throttling is when an ISP purposely slows down your internet speed based on what you are doing online. It is a reactive measure employed in communication networks to regulate network traffic.

Throttling can be used to actively limit a user's upload and download rates when the user is deemed to be doing something suspicious. Protocols can be put into place to prevent certain types of network usage on a computer, which would prevent activities like hacking, phishing, etc. It can even be used on programs such as video streaming, BitTorrent protocols and other file sharing applications.
